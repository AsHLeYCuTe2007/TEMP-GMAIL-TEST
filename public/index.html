Quick Answers About Temp Mail

Quick answers about using temp mail on this page.

What Is Temp Mail?

Temp mail is a disposable email service that lets you receive emails without using your personal inbox. With Tmailor, the inbox is temporary, while the address can be reopened later if you saved your access token.

Can Temp Mail Receive OTP and Verification Codes?

Yes. Temp mail can receive many OTPs, verification emails, and sign-up messages. However, some websites may block disposable email domains, so delivery is not guaranteed on every service.

Can I Reuse a Temp Mail Address?

Yes. If you save your access token, you can reopen the same temp mail address later and use it again. Without the token, you may lose access after closing the session or switching devices.

How Long Do Emails Stay in the Inbox?

Emails stay in the inbox for 24 hours. After that, messages are automatically removed, even if you still have access to the same email address.

Can I Recover a Temp Mail Inbox After Closing the Browser?

Yes, if you saved your access token. The token lets you reopen the same temp mail address and view any messages that are still within the 24-hour retention period.

Does Tmailor Allow Sending Emails?

No. Tmailor is a receive-only temp mail service. You can receive incoming emails, but you cannot send outbound messages from a Tmailor address.

Are Tmailor Domains Blocked by Websites?

Sometimes. Tmailor uses a large pool of domains and Google-backed mail infrastructure to improve delivery, but some websites may still block temporary email domains.
